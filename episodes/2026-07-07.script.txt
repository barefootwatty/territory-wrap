The Territory Wrap — Tuesday 7 July 2026.

G'day Watty, good morning. It's Tuesday the seventh of July, another cracker of a dry-season day in the Top End. Here's your Territory Wrap — the good news from the bush, the boat and the bar-mouth. It's a leaner batch than usual today, the fresh news was a bit quiet, but it's all worth knowing.

Here's what's in today's wrap. Christmas Island still delivering the goods on the flats; Aussie-made lures and flies star at this month's Expo; Victoria's next 10-million-fish stocking season kicks off; Queensland opens up more dams for stocked fishing.

Aus Rec Fishing.

A good one for the lure and fly tragics now. The Australian Lure, Fly and Outdoors Expo is on this coming weekend, the eleventh and twelfth of July, at the Fernvale Showgrounds in Queensland. It's a celebration of Aussie-made gear — timber, plastic and metal lures, wet flies, dry flies, even old vintage collectables to trade. Fifteen bucks in for adults, free for the kids under sixteen. Well worth a look if you're that way inclined.

Down south, the fish stocking just keeps on coming. Victoria is rolling into a new season from this month, with the Fisheries Authority planning to put more than ten million fish into around two hundred and forty-seven waters between now and June next year. Everything from Murray cod and trout to threatened species like the Macquarie perch. More fish, more waters, more spots to wet a line.

And staying with more fish in the water — Queensland's stocked-dam scheme has ticked over twenty-five years and grown to sixty-six dams and weirs. New waters coming online include Paradise Dam, now stocked with bass and barramundi, along with Mount Morgan and Enoggera. More stocked impoundments means more places to chase a fish closer to home. A good problem to have.

International & Biz.

First up, one for the travel side of things. A fresh write-up from Christmas Island — Kiritimati, out in the Pacific — paints the usual picture: bonefish everywhere, giant trevally that make you earn every one, and that relentless wind. The angler's take was a good one: it's hunting, not harvesting. You back your own skills and the island rewards you. Still a cracking destination, and the lodges have stayed busy right through the year.

And that's your wrap. A quiet news morning, but plenty still ticking along nicely out there. Have a good one, look after the crew, and we'll see you next run. Tight lines.

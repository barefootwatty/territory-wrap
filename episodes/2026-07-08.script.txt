Rec Fishing & the Bodies.

First up, a good-news story from down south. The Trophy Hunter Snapper Bash ran out of Coffs Harbour on the New South Wales mid-north coast, and it pulled a huge field — a hundred and fifty-four boats, all chasing a prize pool worth more than two hundred thousand dollars. It's run by a couple of blokes, Sondal Bensan and Mike Bonnici, who took over from the old Dave Irvine Snapper Classic and rebuilt it as a team comp. By all accounts it's less about the cash and more about the good yarns and the camaraderie — phone calls between drifts, a beer at the pub after stop-fishing, families making a week of it. The winners this year, Tai Donelan and his crew, blew the field away with a bag of ten cracking snapper. Sounds like a bloody good week on the water.

Fly Fishing.

And here's one for the fly crew. The blokes at Gink and Gasoline have put up a short video breaking down the double haul — that's the cast that gives you the line speed and the distance, and there's nowhere it matters more than in the salt. A lot of anglers find the double haul a bit of a mystery, so if that's you, it's well worth a watch on the drive home. Get that cast dialled in and you'll be putting the fly where the barra, the queenies and the trevally actually are.

And that's the lot for today. Short and sweet, but all worth knowing. Look after the crew, get out on the water if you can, and we'll catch you next run. Tight lines.

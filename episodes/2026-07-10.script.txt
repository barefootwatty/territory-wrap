Rec Fishing & the Bodies.

First up, a good one out of New South Wales. There's a brand-new artificial reef been sunk off Coffs Harbour, built to boost the rec fishing and a bit of coastal tourism along with it. It's a serious bit of kit — two steel structures, each about fifteen and a half metres square and twelve metres tall, weighing fifty tonnes apiece. They've been dropped around five hundred metres apart in twenty-eight metres of water, about ten kilometres south of Coffs. The whole thing was designed by naval architects and biologists to cop a one-in-a-hundred-year storm and keep sitting there, quietly turning into prime habitat for mackerel, snapper, mulloway and baitfish. It's the fourteenth of these reefs now along the New South Wales coast, paid for out of the Recreational Fishing Trust — so that's your fishing licence money going straight back into more fish. Good to see.

Fly Fishing.

And to round it out, a bit of fly inspiration for the drive home. Flylords ran a clip following an Aussie bloke, Mathew Scholz — he's the fella behind the Pelagic Pursuit channel — sight-casting to big barra on the fly. It's the good stuff: fish jammed up tight in heavy structure, big rods, and once he hooks up it turns into an absolute tug-of-war with one of the angriest fish in the country.

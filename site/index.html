New artificial reef sunk off Coffs to fire up the fishing

A new artificial reef has been installed off Coffs Harbour on the NSW Mid North Coast to boost sustainable recreational fishing and coastal tourism. It's made up of two 50-tonne steel structures, each 15.6 by 15.6 metres and 12 metres high, engineered by naval architects and biologists to hold up in a one-in-100-year storm and placed about 500 metres apart in 28 metres of water, roughly 10km south of Coffs. Designed to draw mackerel, snapper, mulloway and baitfish, it's the latest of 14 offshore reefs now along the NSW coast, funded through the Recreational Fishing Trust and built by SMC Marine.

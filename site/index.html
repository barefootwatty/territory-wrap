Bureau of Meteorology declares El Niño underway, monsoon onset likely delayed in the north

Bureau of Meteorology declares El Niño underway, monsoon onset likely delayed in the north

The Bureau of Meteorology has declared that El Niño is now underway in the tropical Pacific and is likely to persist into next year, with some forecast models pointing to one of the strongest events on record. For northern Australia, including the Top End, El Niño typically means a delayed monsoon onset, below average spring rainfall and warmer than average days, alongside an increased fire risk further south.

New artificial reef installed off Coffs Harbour to boost fish stocks

New artificial reef installed off Coffs Harbour to boost fish stocks

Two new steel artificial reef structures have been installed about two kilometres south-west of Sawtell and ten kilometres south of Coffs Harbour, each with a footprint of 15.6 by 15.6 metres, standing 12 metres tall and weighing 50 tonnes. Designed by naval architects, coastal engineers and biologists, the reefs are built to support mackerel, snapper, mulloway and baitfish, and join twelve other artificial reefs already installed along the New South Wales coast. The project was funded through the Recreational Fishing Trust and the Marine Estate Management Strategy.

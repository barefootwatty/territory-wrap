Idaho's South Fork Snake River fishing best at dawn and dusk as heat builds

Idaho's South Fork Snake River fishing best at dawn and dusk as heat builds

Fly anglers on Idaho's South Fork Snake River are being advised to fish early and late as a heatwave pushes water temperatures up and air temperatures toward 100 degrees Fahrenheit. With flows running high out of Palisades Dam, guides report the best dry fly fishing is in the first two hours of daylight, before wind picks up, with a short but productive evening caddis rise later on. Through the heat of the day, fish are holding deep and tight to cover, favouring nymphs fished along the river's main channel edges.

The bass tournament near-misses that live on in fishing folklore

The bass tournament near-misses that live on in fishing folklore

A look back at some of competitive bass fishing's most agonising near-misses shows just how fine the margins can be. In the 1989 Bassmaster Classic, angler Jim Bitter lost the title by two ounces after a fish he was measuring flopped off his boat and back into the water. Eight years later, Dalton Bobo missed out on the same title by a single ounce after a dead-fish penalty. Writer Shaye Baker, who lost second place at a 2013 tournament over an accidental extra fish in his livewell, says the stories still resonate with anglers decades on.

New fish habitat project gives tournament bass a place to recover

New fish habitat project gives tournament bass a place to recover

A new artificial habitat zone has been installed at Oklahoma's Lake Eufaula to help bass recover after tournament weigh-ins. Major League Fishing's Fisheries Management Division worked with state wildlife authorities and a local equipment dealer to anchor a network of long-lasting habitat structures at the mouth of Eufaula Cove, replacing the cedar trees traditionally used for such projects, which break down over time. The zone gives pressured fish shelter to reorient before being released back into the main lake, and builds on a similar project completed with a local high school fishing team in 2024.

New artificial reef off Coffs Harbour to boost fish habitat

New artificial reef off Coffs Harbour to boost fish habitat

A new artificial reef has been installed off Coffs Harbour on the New South Wales mid-north coast, using two steel structures weighing fifty tonnes each, set in twenty-eight metres of water about five hundred metres apart. Funded by the Recreational Fishing Trust and the Marine Estate Management Strategy and built by marine firm SMC Marine, it is designed to attract mackerel, snapper, mulloway and baitfish. It becomes the thirteenth artificial reef deployed along the New South Wales coast.

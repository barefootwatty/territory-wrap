Christmas Island still delivering the goods on the flats

Christmas Island still delivering the goods on the flats

A fresh trip report from Kiritimati — the Pacific flats fishery Angling Adventures sends anglers to — describes endless bonefish, hard-won giant trevally and that famously relentless wind. The writer's verdict: it's hunting, not harvesting, and it rewards anglers who back their own skills. Lodges and hosted trips remained busy through 2026.

Aussie-made lures and flies star at this month's Expo

Aussie-made lures and flies star at this month's Expo

The Australian Lure, Fly & Outdoors Expo — a celebration of Aussie-made lures, lure-making and fly-tying — runs 11 to 12 July at Fernvale Showgrounds in Queensland. Expect timber, plastic and metal lures, wet and dry flies, vintage collectables to trade, plus rods, reels and gear. Entry is $15 for adults and free for under-16s with a paying adult.

Victoria's next 10-million-fish stocking season kicks off

Victoria's next 10-million-fish stocking season kicks off

Victoria's record fish-stocking run rolls into a new season from this July, with the Victorian Fisheries Authority's draft plan to release more than 10 million fish across around 247 waters through to June 2027 — from Murray cod and trout to conservation species like Macquarie perch. More fish in more waters for anglers right across the state.

Source: Victorian Fisheries Authority

Queensland opens up more dams for stocked fishing

Queensland opens up more dams for stocked fishing

Queensland's Stocked Impoundment Permit Scheme has hit its 25th year and expanded to 66 dams and weirs, with new waters including Paradise Dam (bass and barramundi), Mount Morgan No. 7 Dam and Enoggera Dam now stocked. More stocked impoundments means more places to chase a fish closer to home.

Source: Fishing Charters Australia

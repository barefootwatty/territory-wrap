Z-Man Fishing launches sinking Kingpin and Stuntman soft lures

Z-Man Fishing launches sinking Kingpin and Stuntman soft lures

Tackle maker Z-Man Fishing has introduced a new sinking formulation of its signature ElaZtech material, debuting in two bass lures: the Kingpin soft stickbait and the Stuntman soft jerkbait. Unlike traditional salt-weighted soft plastics, the density is built directly into the polymer, giving a sink rate that stays consistent no matter how much the bait is fished. Both lures are made in the United States and reach retailers this coming autumn, priced at around seven US dollars each.

Visiting Georgia angler reels in new Idaho state record brown trout

Georgia angler Caroline Langdale was fishing Idaho's iconic South Fork Snake River with guide Ed Emory of South Fork Lodge when she hooked a brown trout that has since been certified as a new Idaho catch-and-release state record. The fish was measured and released unharmed, with Langdale telling Idaho Fish and Game she never expected the fish of a lifetime that day.

Ohio farm-pond angler lands new state record green sunfish

Ohio farm-pond angler lands new state record green sunfish

Pioneer, Ohio angler Jeret Stevens set out to catch a feed of fish for dinner but instead landed a state record. Fishing a farm pond near his parents' home with a simple worm-and-bobber rig, the 38-year-old professional chef hooked a green sunfish that certified scales weighed at 1.35 pounds. The Ohio Department of Natural Resources has confirmed it as the new state record, eclipsing a mark that had stood since 2018.

US states rally anglers to help stop the spread of invasive species

US states rally anglers to help stop the spread of invasive species

State wildlife agencies across the United States are enlisting recreational anglers in the fight against invasive fish species this month. Wisconsin's two-week Great Lakes Landing Blitz has volunteers greeting boaters at ramps with clean, drain and dry advice, while Colorado and Maryland are running cash-incentive tournaments targeting invasive pike and blue catfish, and Florida's Lionfish Challenge rewards anglers for every lionfish they remove from state waters.
